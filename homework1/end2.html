You break into a run.

Instinct drives you forward. The black birds above you flocked; a giant arrow piercing the skies, shooting ever faster ahead. You follow them, or rather, the vague dark cloud of their silhouettes, and push pass bark, wood, rock — only to find yourself approaching a very familiar clearing.

This is...

You are stumped. You are back at where you awoke, the grass and scattered trees a familiar sight. As the confusion sets in, it dawns on you that you have no idea where you are. You cannot remember anything. But with the crows flying further, and the presence behind you nearer, you cannot help but push aside your muddled thoughts. You have to act. Two options — you can once again choose to run, or you can forsake your instinct of flight and stay behind. Which will you choose?
